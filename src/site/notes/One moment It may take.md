Sixteen years ago the myth of Plato's Atlantis found its way into the real world. This was the name of Alexander McQueen’s 2010 Spring/Summer womenswear collection and runway show. He used the story as a conceptual frame for imagining a future world shaped by flooding and environmental collapse. McQueen also connected that idea to Darwinian evolution. Garments were designed to give the appearance of reptiles and sea creatures, including snakes, stingrays, and jellyfish, while models were made to look hybrid or post-human, with fins, scales, shells, and other aquatic attributes. It was a proposition that humanity might adapt by evolving back toward aquatic life. The human body, pressed by rising seas, begins to assume new and unsettling forms. The collection was structured as a kind of visual evolution, moving from land and reptile references toward sea-creature forms and aquatic color palettes. One of the first major runway shows to be livestreamed online, it opens with a projected movie of a nude woman lying in sand. Soon snakes begin to appear, and she is covered by them. With a slightly erotic undertone, you almost get the sense that she is enraptured, making love to the reptiles. Next a psychedelic animation covers the screen, making it seem as though you had woken up in a pit of them yourself. All of a sudden, all sorts of truly avant garde(n) women begin to appear. Incredibly serious demeanor paired with quite lavish clothing designs, and otherworldly makeup/hair, it is like another species rose from the waters - to the runway. They strut as though just coming back into the house after that thanksgiving "walk" with the cousins, taken to help dinner digest. Really a beautiful show, I found myself in awe of the shoes they were wearing, what became the most remembered relics of the occasion. The "Armadillo." Only 24 exist across the planet today. 21 were originally designed for the show, and then 3 more were recreated for a charity event which took place in 2015. They were so tall that some models refused to walk in them. Lady Gaga wore the pair in her Bad Romance video, and it was at this Plato's Atlantis runway show that the song was first premiered. It turns out that this was a turbulent time in Alexanders life. The original plan was to have a box on stage, and then, at the end, walk down the runway himself, open it up, and end the show with a "bang." He couldn't go through with it. McQueen held onto all of these emotions for about a year or so before he ended up committing the act the day before his mothers funeral. There was a mixture of cocaine, sleeping pills, and also a smaller amount of tranquillizers found in his system, but had hung himself.
