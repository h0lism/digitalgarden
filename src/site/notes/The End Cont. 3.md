Gravity from the Sun and Moon pulls on Earth's equatorial bulge (Earth is wider at the equator aka its midpoint/bellybutton). This tug tries to "straighten" Earth up, but because Earth is spinning, it causes the axis to wobble instead. Because of this, a rotating object like the Earth tends to form a spheroid egg type shape rather than a perfect sphere. This is of course all theoretical, and only true if the Earth is not flat. It takes approximately 25,772 years (often rounded to 26,000) for the axis to complete one full circle. This is known as the "Great Year" or "Platonic Year." As Earth wobbles through that 26,000-year cycle, its axis draws a sort of cone shape in space. If you measure from the center of the cone to the edge, it is 23.5 degrees. If you measure the full wobble width (from one side of the circle to the opposite side), it gives you 47 degrees.
